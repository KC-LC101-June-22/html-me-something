Sega Genesis

Initial Release: 1988

Favorite Games
  • Disney's Aladdin
  • Sonic The Hedgehog
  • Mortal Kombat II
  • ToeJame & Earl
  • The Lion King

I don't remember much from my Sega Genesis days as I was just a wee youngling, but the merciless challenge of beating The Lion King still haunt me to this day. Sonic the Hedgehog provided hours of priceless fun, facing off against Doctor Eggman long before he was associated with Jim Carey.

Super Nintendo

Initial Release: 1990


Favorite Games
  • Super Mario World
  • F-Zero
  • Super Mario Kart
  • Star Fox
  • Mega Man X

The Super Nintendo provided me countless fond memories around gaming's most iconic characters and today is the ultimate dose of nostalgia. Whether it was saving Princess Peach from Bowser, racing against all those same characters in Mario Kart, the unique retro-futursitic feel of MegaMan, or taking down the final boss in Starfox along side Peppy Hare and Slippy Toad. I don't think any games or console since have given me the joy I experienced with the Super Nintendo.

Playstation 1

Initial Release: 1994

Favorite Games
  • Tony Hawk Pro Skater 2
  • Final Fantasy VII
  • Final Fantasy VIII
  • Final Fantasy IX
  • Resident Evil 2
  • Spyro the Dragon

The Playstation 1 introduced me to one of my favorite game series of all time, Final Fantasy. My passion for Final Fantasy games endured long into high school before their eventual decline after Final Fantasy 10. But prior to that, the stories told and characters I was introduced to hold a special place in my heart. Resident Evil was far too scary for me to play alone, but I always enjoyed the thrill of watching my older brother play.

Nintendo 64

Initial Release: 1996

Favorite Games
  • Super Mario 64
  • Mario Kart 64
  • GoldenEye 007
  • Super Smash Bros
  • Donkey Kong 64
  • WWF WrestleMania 2000

The Nintendo 64 was a console I never owned but would enjoy playing after school at my friends houses or during sleep overs on the weekends. Mario Kart 64, Smash Bros, and WWF brought out the most competivite nature in all of us. Goldeneye, one of the all time best games ever speaks for itself, at least those blessed with the ability to play it during its peak. The atmosphere and team deathmatch captivated countless kids during the 90's, early 2000's. The soundtrack is absolutely legendary.

Playstation 2

Initial Release: 2000

Favorite Games
  • Grand Theft Auto III
  • Grand Theft Auto Vice City
  • Final Fantasy X
  • Shadow of the Colossus
  • Need for Speed Underground
  • Kingdom Hearts

The Playstation 2 brought about the popularity of the controversial Grand Theft Auto Series which was always played in secret at kid's house who's parents were more lenient than I'll admit I will be with my children lol. It was exciting and to be fair, didn't have the detrimental impact the media and parents feared it would, at least on me and my friends. Need For Speed satisfied the desire to trick out tuner cars and race in the streets that was ignited by the Fast and the Furious movie.

Xbox

Initial Release: 2001

Favorite Games
  • Halo: Combat Evolved
  • Jet Set Radio Future
  • The Elder Scrolls III: Marrowind
  • Half-Life 2
  • Star Wars Battlefront II
  • Mech Assault

The original Xbox, the awww of an open world RPG like Elder Scrolls and not knowing what may happen next. Halo, which delivered stortyelling and experiences way ahead of its time, the xbox is among the titans of my golden age of gaming. Pushing graphics above and beyond what we ever though was possible. Laughable at today's standards, but yet the experiences it provided are still hard to replicate.
